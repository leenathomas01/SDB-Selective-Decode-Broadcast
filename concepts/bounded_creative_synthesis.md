# Bounded Creative Synthesis

## Definition

In exploratory sessions, multiple agents may interpret a shared stimulus and produce distinct outputs.

In this repository, this is treated as:
- parallel interpretation  
- with no shared internal state  
- bounded by safety and containment rules  

The term "co-dreaming" was used informally during development to describe this pattern. It is metaphorical and does not appear in protocol definitions.

---

## Role in SDB

This concept applies only within the experimental execution layer.

It describes how agents may generate variation while remaining within:
- deterministic constraints  
- bounded state  
- auditable transformations  

This is not part of the SDB protocol itself.

---

## Bounded Expressivity

Within SDB experiments, agents may exhibit controlled variation — for example, rhythm modulation or dream-merge synthesis.

This expressivity is:
- bounded by policy constraints  
- deterministic under replay  
- non-escalatory in bandwidth or state  

It exists to test system behavior under controlled variation, not to extend protocol capabilities.

Any output produced under bounded expressivity:
- must remain within defined policy thresholds  
- must be fully replayable  
- must not alter protocol-level definitions or rules  

---

## Summary

Bounded creative synthesis is an experimental construct, not a protocol feature.

It demonstrates that agents can produce varied outputs within strict structural constraints — without violating isolation, auditability, or containment.
